La Oroya – Doe Run Peru (DRP)

Sign up now for La Oroya News!

Doe Run Peru (DRP) and its renowned parent-corporation in the Missouri lead-belt purchased the La Oroya multi-metal smelting facility in 1997. This complex is a tremendous source of heavy metal and sulfur dioxide emissions, and is to blame for severe public health problems in the poor, Andean city where it is located. Respiratory problems caused by sulfur dioxide contamination and lead poisoning are particularly widespread. In fact, according to a 1999 study by the Ministry of Health, 99.1% of the children in La Oroya suffered from lead poisoning while nearly 20% needed urgent hospitalization. Unfortunately, little medical care is available, and even if it were, medical treatment would mean little as long the reckless environmental contamination continues. Because lead inhibits neurological development in children, the thousands of children poisoned by the smelter will be impaired for life.

Sadly, even after seeing these shocking results in 1999, the Peruvian Ministry of Health took no action to treat the children of La Oroya, limit exposure, or educate the public about the health risks. Numerous subsequent environmental and health studies have shown that the situation in La Oroya remains critical. This is not surprising, considering environmental conditions in the city.

Since 1998, AIDA has educated local NGOs, government agencies, and the affected community regarding the contamination and health problems the city confronts. In 2002, we published “La Oroya Cannot Wait” which helped kick-start the international campaign to protect health in La Oroya. Throughout our efforts, we work to ensure that government authorities, the company, and civil society all take a constructive and proactive role in finding solutions to address the public health disaster in La Oroya.
